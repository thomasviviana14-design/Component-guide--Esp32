ESP32 Microcontroller

The ESP32 Board: A powerful dual-core IoT solution

Introduction

The ESP32 is a low-cost, low-power "System on a Chip" with integrated Wi-Fi and dual-mode Bluetooth. Think of it as a much faster, more capable version of the Arduino that can connect to the internet right out of the box. It is designed for mobile devices, wearable electronics, and Internet of Things (IoT) applications.

How it Works

Inside the chip is a Dual-Core processor. One core can handle your Wi-Fi connection and data security, while the other core runs your specific code (like reading a sensor). It takes electricity and follows the instructions you write in languages like C++ or Python to control the electronic pins on its sides.

How it is Used

Because it is small and wireless, it is used for home automation (controlling lights with your phone), industrial monitoring (checking machine temperature remotely), and even simple robotics. It is the perfect bridge between physical hardware and the digital internet.

Understanding the Pins

Each pin on the ESP32 acts as a gateway. Here is a simple breakdown of what they do:

  • EN: The Reset button used to restart your program.
  • VCC / 5V: The main power input pin for the board.
  • 3.3V: Power output pin to provide steady voltage to sensors.
  • GND: The common ground to complete your electrical circuits.
  • GPIO: General pins for connecting buttons, LEDs, and sensors.
  • ADC: Reads analog signals (like light or temperature levels).
  • DAC: Sends out true analog voltage signals.
  • Touch: Pins that detect human touch without mechanical buttons.
  • TX / RX: Communication pins for talking to your computer.
